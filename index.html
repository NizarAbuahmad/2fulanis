Our Story

From Guinea's Fields
to Your Kitchen

Two Fulanis is the consumer brand of Two Fulanis, founded in Guinea — the world's leading producer of fonio. Our mission is to bring this exceptional grain to international tables while keeping its roots firmly in the communities that have grown it for generations.

The brand is named for its two Fulani sisters: Oumou Sow, who leads operations from Guinea and the United States, and Saran Sow, who drives international markets from Dubai and across Asia. Together they combine deep agricultural knowledge with global business reach.

Our fonio is grown on our own farmland in the Fouta Djallon and Faranah Region of Guinea, harvested by hand, then cleaned, steamed, dried and packaged in our fully operational processing facility in Forécariah — all to international food safety standards.

Our Farm in Guinea

From the Fields of the Fouta Djallon & Faranah Region

Our 20+ hectares of farmland in the Fouta Djallon and Faranah Region of Guinea produce some of the finest fonio in the world — harvested by hand, processed in our state-of-the-art facility in Forécariah, and packed for your table.

Step 1 — Harvest

Our farmers hand-harvest ripe fonio stalks during the dry season in the Fouta Djallon and Faranah Region.

Step 2 — Bundle & Dry

Stalks are bundled and sun-dried in the field before threshing — a critical step for clean, odour-free grain.

Step 3 — Thresh & Clean

Threshed grain is brought to our facility where it is cleaned, stone-free and odour-free, ready for steaming and packaging.

Step 4 — Process & Pack

Every batch is cleaned, steamed, dried and packaged to international food safety standards in our Forécariah facility.

Our Operational Facility
Forécariah, Guinea

Our facility in Forécariah is fully operational, processing up to 1,000 kg of fonio per hour. Every batch is cleaned, steamed, dried and packaged to international food safety standards — stone-free, odour-free, and consistently excellent.

Why Fonio

The Grain That Does Everything

For centuries fonio fed West Africa's emperors and farmers alike. Now the world is catching up — and for good reason.

🌾

Gluten Free

Naturally free of gluten — ideal for celiac sufferers and anyone seeking a clean grain alternative to wheat or barley.

📉

Low Glycemic

One of the lowest GI grains available. Slower energy release means fewer spikes, better focus, and sustained fuel.

💪

Nutrient Dense

Rich in iron, zinc, B-vitamins, and essential amino acids that many other grains — including quinoa — lack.

⚡

Ready in 5 Min

One of the fastest-cooking grains in the world. Boil, steam, or toast — on the table in minutes.

Two Fulani Sisters

Two Fulani Sisters.
One Mission.

Founder

Oumou Sow

Oumou Sow is the founder of Two Fulanis — a visionary entrepreneur with over two decades of healthcare experience and an MBA-backed business acumen. She founded the brand to bridge the gap between Guinea's extraordinary agricultural heritage and the growing global demand for clean, nutritious food.

Operating across continents with dedicated teams in Guinea and the United States, Oumou has established our farmland in the Fouta Djallon and Faranah Region, and our fully operational processing facility in Forécariah — processing up to 1,000 kg of fonio per hour.

Her mission is dual: bring the world the best fonio it has ever tasted, and ensure the Guinean farmers who grow it are fairly rewarded and sustainably supported.

Oumou Sow — Founder & CEO, Two Fulanis

Co-Founder

Saran Sow

Saran Sow is a co-founder of Two Fulanis and a seasoned entrepreneur with deep roots in African culture and commerce. As founder of Afro Baobab — an African art, culture, education and event planning business — she has spent years building bridges between African heritage and global audiences.

Renowned for orchestrating events showcasing African cuisine — particularly fonio-based dishes — Saran co-founded Two Fulanis to take this mission further: bringing premium Guinean fonio to kitchens across Dubai, Asia and beyond.

With five years of specialised experience in African food and agriculture, Saran leads all international sales and marketing for Two Fulanis — opening doors across the Gulf, Asia and the African diaspora worldwide.

Saran Sow — Co-Founder, Two Fulanis
